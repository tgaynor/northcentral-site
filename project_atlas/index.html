Airfield access

Where the campus meets the runway.

The parcel's defining advantage is its position against Payson Municipal Airport (KPAN) and its 5,504-foot runwayi. The concept anticipates a future connection from campus apron to taxiway.

The parcel is eligible for a through-the-fence agreement — a direct campus-to-taxiway connection to Payson Municipal Airport. Not yet secured, but eligible.

Workforce & quality of life

A place people want to build — and stay.

Payson pairs a high-country setting with proximity to metropolitan Phoenix, giving an aerospace employer access to talent, training partners and a quality of life that helps retain it.

The campus concept includes an on-site training academy to develop technicians and machinists locally, in partnership with regional education providers — reducing reliance on relocation to staff skilled roles.

Important disclosures

This page presents an illustrative concept for a proposed aerospace campus. No site plan has been approved. All renderings, layouts, zones, phasing, areas and metrics are conceptual and provided for evaluation only.

Parcel boundaries and acreage (APN 302-39-096J, 14.9 acres) are approximate and subject to survey and title verification. Utility capacity and connection details for power, water, wastewater, natural gas and telecommunications require verification with the respective providers.

The parcel is eligible for a through-the-fence agreement with Payson Municipal Airport; such an agreement is not yet secured. Any airfield access requires Town of Payson and FAA coordination and approval.

This material is independently prepared and does not constitute an endorsement, approval, or commitment by the Town of Payson, the Arizona Commerce Authority, Arizona Public Service (APS), or any utility or airport authority.
