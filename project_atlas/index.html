The land

Optimal zoning & proximity.

The strongest case for this site is the site itself: how it’s zoned, what already runs through it, and where it sits.

Zoned M-2 for the work

The parcel is zoned M-2 (General Industrial) under Payson’s Unified Development Code §154i, which permits aircraft hangars, manufacturing and warehousing outright.

M-2 · General Industrial

Utilities already run through it

Gas, power, telecom and water mains already run through the parcel, with no speculative extension to fund. Capacity and connection sizing confirm at design.

Mains run through the lot

Close to everything that matters

Residential

Relocating senior engineers, program managers and plant leadership is usually the hardest part of siting advanced manufacturing in a small town. Payson answers it with an established single-family market and premier high-end living communities, with real inventory and a supply balance that favors buyers, while sitting 75 minutes from the largest metro in Arizona.

Single-family market

Homes in Payson

A small market with real depth: hundreds of active single-family listings against a town of roughly 16,700, and better than six months of standing inventory. New building is largely custom and infill rather than tract subdivision, with the high end concentrated inside the gated clubs.

  • Inventory518 active single-family listingsi
  • Supply6.5 months, buyer-favorable
  • Club custom$1.2M to $2.5M+ inside the gates
Gated golf community

Chaparral Pines

A private, gated community built around an 18-hole, par-72 course designed by former U.S. Open and PGA champion David Graham with Scottsdale architect Gary Panks. Its 27,000-square-foot clubhouse carries rough-hewn timber beams, stone fireplaces and sweeping patios.

  • Golf18 holes, par 72 · Graham & Panksi
  • Clubhouse27,000 SF, mountain-lodge character
  • AmenitiesDining, wellness, racquet sports, aquatics
  • SettingPonderosa pine, Mogollon Rim country
Gated golf community

The Rim Club

555 gated acres just above 5,000 feet, wrapped around a Weiskopf-Morrish course, the pair’s final collaboration, that threads ponderosa pine and granite outcroppings with Mogollon Rim views from tees and greens.

  • Golf18 holes · Weiskopf-Morrishi
  • Community555 acres above 5,000 ft
  • ClubhouseRenovated dining, bar, fire-pit patio
  • WellnessFitness center with saunas

Both clubs are operated by Arcis Golf, and a dual membership carries access to both properties: two courses, multiple dining venues, fitness centers, tennis and pickleball. For a campus recruiting nationally, that is a housing and lifestyle package a town this size does not normally offer.

Important disclosures

This page presents an illustrative concept for a proposed aerospace campus. No site plan has been approved. All renderings, layouts, zones, phasing, areas and metrics are conceptual and provided for evaluation only.

Parcel boundaries and acreage (APN 302-39-096J, 14.9 acres) are approximate and subject to survey and title verification. Utility capacity and connection details for power, water, wastewater, natural gas and telecommunications require verification with the respective providers.

The parcel is eligible for a through-the-fence agreement with Payson Municipal Airport. Any airfield access requires Town of Payson and FAA coordination and approval.

Chaparral Pines and The Rim Club are independent private communities with no affiliation to, or involvement in, this project.
